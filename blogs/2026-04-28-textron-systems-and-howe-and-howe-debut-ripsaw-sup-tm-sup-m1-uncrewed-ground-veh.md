Textron Systems And Howe & Howe Debut RIPSAW<sup>TM</sup>  M1 Uncrewed Ground Vehicle (UGV) Technology Demonstrator Article Description <h2 class="pr_releasetitle">Textron Systems And Howe & Howe Debut RIPSAW<sup>TM</sup>  M1 Uncrewed Ground Vehicle (UGV) Technology Demonstrator</h2><p><strong>2026 Modern Day Marine Exposition, Washington D.C. (April 28, 2026) </strong>&ndash; Textron Systems Corporation, a Textron Inc. (NYSE:TXT) company, and its subsidiary Howe &amp; Howe Inc., today debuted the RIPSAW<sup>TM</sup> M1 uncrewed ground vehicle (UGV) technology demonstrator at the 2026 Modern Day Marine exposition. The M1 UGV is designed to support U.S. Marine Corps missions of littoral mobility and uncrewed teaming concept of operations (CONOPS).</p> <p><img style="float:left; padding: 6px 10px 6px 6px;" src="https://s206.q4cdn.com/111183019/files/images/2026/M1-Unveil-Teaser-Image470x320.png">The M1 technology demonstrator is the next iteration in the company&rsquo;s Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA)-designed UGVs, demonstrating the capacity and capability to scale our family of UGVs. The technology demonstrator is the first variant in the M1 line of vehicles, designed as a robotic force multiplier for the Advanced Reconnaissance Vehicle (ARV) and Amphibious Combat Vehicle (ACV) to support Force Design 2030.</p> <p>The UGV can support target missions including hard kill counter-unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) and reconnaissance, surveillance and target acquisition (RSTA). It can also serve as a loitering munition launch platform. The vehicle is envisioned to extend the reach and lethality of the Marines&rsquo; existing systems to keep personnel out of harm&rsquo;s way and protect heavier, and more expensive, ground vehicles.</p> <p>&ldquo;The M1 technology demonstrator shows the art of the possible for how UGVs can support the Marine Corps&rsquo; missions,&rdquo; said Vice President of Programs, Sara Willett. &ldquo;Along with Howe &amp; Howe, we took our experience in autonomous robotic systems across land, air and sea &ndash; the same domains the Marine Corps&rsquo; missions live - to develop this system that demonstrates our ability to scale the SWAP up or down, all while maintaining the common robotic core that enables our UGVs to provide exceptional transportability and battlefield agility.&rdquo;</p> <p>With decades of uncrewed systems leadership and millions of operational hours across domains, Textron Systems continues to draw on its deep experience to deliver reliable, mission&#8209;ready innovation. With a commitment to advancing modular, autonomous technologies that strengthen warfighter effectiveness, Textron Systems continues to shape the future of uncrewed ground operations.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p><strong>ABOUT HOWE &amp; HOWE</strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p> <p>Howe &amp; Howe is a leader in advanced robotic land vehicles, built and proven for extreme conditions.
