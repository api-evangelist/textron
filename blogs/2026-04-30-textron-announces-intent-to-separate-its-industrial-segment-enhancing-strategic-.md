Textron Announces Intent to Separate its Industrial Segment, Enhancing Strategic Focus as a Pure-Play Aerospace and Defense Company Article Description <h2 class="pr_releasetitle">Textron Announces Intent to Separate its Industrial Segment, Enhancing Strategic Focus as a Pure-Play Aerospace and Defense Company</h2><style> .q4default .bwlistdisc { list-style-type: disc }</style> <div class="q4default"> <p><em>Textron to become a pure-play Aerospace &amp; Defense company aligned to its core franchises of Textron Aviation, Bell, and Textron Systems (&ldquo;New Textron&rdquo;) <br></em><em>Separation of Textron&rsquo;s Industrial segment &ndash; composed of Kautex and Textron Specialized Vehicles (&ldquo;Industrial&rdquo;) <br></em><em>Separation designed to enhance strategic focus and unlock shareholder value</em></p> <p>PROVIDENCE, R.I.--(BUSINESS WIRE)-- Textron Inc. (the "Company" or &ldquo;Textron&rdquo;) (NYSE: TXT) today announced its intent to separate its Industrial segment from the Company&rsquo;s core aerospace and defense businesses to enhance its strategic and operational focus and drive long-term value for stakeholders.</p> <p>Textron intends to explore multiple paths to effect the planned separation of its Industrial segment, including but not limited to a sale of the Industrial businesses or a tax-free separation into a standalone, publicly traded company. The separation results in New Textron becoming a pure-play aerospace and defense company aligned to its core franchises of Textron Aviation, Bell, and Textron Systems.</p> <p>&ldquo;This planned separation creates greater clarity and focus for both businesses,&rdquo; said Lisa M. Atherton, Chief Executive Officer of Textron. &ldquo;New Textron will move forward as a pure-play aerospace and defense company positioned for higher growth, while Industrial gains the independence to pursue strategies aligned with its distinct strengths&mdash;unlocking long term value for all stakeholders.&rdquo;</p> <p><strong>Compelling Strategic Rationale for a Separation</strong></p> <p>New Textron and Industrial operate in distinct markets with unique business opportunities and investment requirements. As separate companies, each is expected to benefit from:</p> <ul class="bwlistdisc"> <li><strong>Enhanced agility and focus</strong> to better position for long-term success;</li> <li><strong>Ability to tailor capital allocation strategies</strong> aligned with each company&rsquo;s growth profile, product development activities, and larger industry dynamics;</li> <li><strong>Increased strategic flexibility</strong> to pursue organic and inorganic growth opportunities;</li> <li><strong>Compelling investment profiles</strong> appealing to different investor bases; and</li> <li><strong>Distinct management teams</strong> comprised of experienced industry leaders with relevant expertise and track records of value creation.</li> </ul> <p>&ldquo;Through the Board of Directors&rsquo; strategic planning process and our ongoing portfolio review, the Board and the management team concluded that pursuing a separation of our Industrial segment is the right approach to sharpen the strategic focus of Textron and support long-term value creation for shareholders,&rdquo; said Scott C. Donnelly, Textron&rsquo;s Executive Chairman.</p> <p><strong>New Textron: Premier Pure-Play Aerospace &amp; Defense Company</strong></p> <p>Following the planned separation, New Textron, with over $12 billion in expected 2026 revenues and $19 billion in backlog, will be a premier pure-play aerospace and defense company with world-class engineering capabilities, a long track record of innovation, and proven manufacturing and support operations. The Company will be anchored by its core franchises: in general aviation under the Cessna and Beechcraft brands and in military and commercial rotorcraft under the Bell brand, in addition to Textron Systems&rsquo; differentiated suite of aerospace and defense products and services.</p> <p>New Textron expects a successful separation to increase its revenue growth profile and operating margins. The Company remains committed to maintaining a strong balance sheet and consistent capital allocation priorities, including investments in research and development and capital expenditures.</p> <p><strong>Industrial: A Global Mobility Company</strong></p> <p>Industrial, with over $3 billion in expected 2026 revenues, is composed of Kautex &ndash; a leader in plastic fuel systems, battery enclosures, and clear-vision systems for the automotive industry &ndash; and Textron Specialized Vehicles, a manufacturer of specialized vehicles and equipment with globally recognized brands such as E-Z-GO, PACE Technologies, Jacobsen, and TUG Technologies.</p> <p>"We are confident this next chapter will enable Industrial to build on its strong foundation and deliver enhanced value for employees, customers, and shareholders,&rdquo; said Atherton. She continued, &ldquo;Throughout the separation process, we will remain focused on positioning our talented teams for long-term success."</p> <p><strong>Transaction Details</strong></p> <p>The Company is targeting completion of the separation within 12 to 18 months, subject to the satisfaction of certain conditions customary for such a proposed separation, including receipt of any required regulatory approvals and final approval of the Company&rsquo;s Board of Directors. There can be no assurance regarding the ultimate timing or structure of the proposed separation or that a transaction will be completed.</p> <p>As the Company pursues this separation, Textron will continue to operate its Industrial businesses in alignment with its current strategy, including continued investments in growth, margin improvement, and innovation.</p> <p><strong>First-Quarter 2026 Results and Conference Call</strong></p> <p>In a separate press release issued today, Textron announced its first-quarter results.</p> <p>Textron will host a conference call at 8:00 a.m. (Eastern) to discuss the results and the proposed separation.
